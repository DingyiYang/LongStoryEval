### Review:
**Plot and Structure:**
The story's pacing was uneven, with some sections feeling rushed and others dragging on. The multiple plotlines and character arcs were engaging, but the lack of a clear resolution left me wanting more.

**Characters:**
The characters were well-developed and intriguing, each with their own unique stories and motivations. However, the numerous plotlines and character arcs made it challenging to fully connect with them.

**Writing and Language:**
The writing was engaging and immersive, with vivid descriptions that brought the world to life. However, the pacing issues and lack of a clear resolution detracted from the overall experience.

**World-Building and Setting:**
The Grand Abeona Hotel, a luxurious spaceship, is a captivating setting that immerses readers in a richly detailed world. The hotel's unique features, such as its ability to move between planets and systems, add depth to the narrative.

**Themes:**
The novel explores themes of love, loss, and the complexities of human relationships. It also delves into the consequences of the Empire's actions and the resilience of its inhabitants.

**Emotional Impact:**
The story evoked strong emotions, particularly in the final chapters, which left me feeling both hopeful and melancholic.

**Enjoyment and Engagement:**
I thoroughly enjoyed the novel, despite its flaws. The unique setting and engaging characters kept me invested throughout.

**Expectation Fulfillment:**
I was drawn to this novel by its intriguing premise and the author's reputation. While it didn't fully meet my expectations, it was still a worthwhile read.

### Overall Assessment:
Overall, I enjoyed this novel for its unique setting, engaging characters, and emotional depth. While the pacing issues and lack of a clear resolution were drawbacks, the story's themes and world-building made it a worthwhile read. I would recommend it to fans of science fiction and mystery, but with the caveat that it may not fully satisfy those seeking a more traditional narrative.

### Score: 3.5

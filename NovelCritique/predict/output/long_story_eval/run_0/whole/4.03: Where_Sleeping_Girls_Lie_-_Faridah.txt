### Review:
**Plot and Structure:**
The story's pacing was inconsistent, with some sections feeling rushed and others dragging on. The mystery surrounding Elizabeth's disappearance was intriguing, but the resolution felt rushed and lacked depth.

**Characters:**
Sade's character development was well-executed, and her growth throughout the story was satisfying. However, the supporting characters lacked depth, making it difficult to connect with them on a personal level.

**Writing and Language:**
The writing style was engaging, and the author's use of metaphors and imagery added depth to the narrative.

**World-Building and Setting:**
The boarding school setting was well-crafted, and the author effectively conveyed the isolation and confinement that came with it.

**Themes:**
The novel effectively explores themes of grief, trauma, and the struggle for justice, particularly in the context of a toxic school environment.

**Emotional Impact:**
The story elicited strong emotions, particularly in the portrayal of Sade's grief and her journey toward healing.

**Enjoyment and Engagement:**
The novel was engaging, and I found myself invested in Sade's journey.

**Expectation Fulfillment:**
The novel's premise was intriguing, and I was initially excited to dive into the story.

### Overall Assessment:
Overall, I found the novel to be an engaging and emotionally resonant read, with strong character development and a well-crafted setting. However, the inconsistent pacing and lack of depth in supporting characters held it back from being a truly exceptional story.

### Score: 3.5

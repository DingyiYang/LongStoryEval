### Review:
**Plot and Structure:**
The story's pacing was uneven, with some sections feeling rushed and others dragging on. The plot was predictable, and the twists lacked surprise, making it difficult to stay engaged.

**Characters:**
The characters lacked depth and development, making it challenging to connect with them. Wren's growth felt forced, and her relationship with Lee was underwhelming.

**Writing and Language:**
The writing style was inconsistent, with some sections feeling juvenile and others overly dramatic. The dialogue often came across as cheesy and unrealistic.

**World-Building and Setting:**
The world-building was lacking, and the magical system was poorly explained, leaving me confused about how the magic worked.

**Themes:**
The story's themes of love and self-discovery were underdeveloped and lacked depth.

**Emotional Impact:**
The story failed to evoke any significant emotions, leaving me feeling indifferent.

**Enjoyment and Engagement:**
I found myself bored and disconnected from the story, unable to stay engaged.

**Expectation Fulfillment:**
I was initially excited about the book, but my expectations were quickly dashed.

### Overall Assessment:
Overall, I was disappointed with this book. The uneven pacing, lack of character development, and poor world-building made it a struggle to finish. The story's themes and emotional impact were underwhelming, and the writing style was inconsistent. I wouldn't recommend this book to anyone.

### Score: 1.5

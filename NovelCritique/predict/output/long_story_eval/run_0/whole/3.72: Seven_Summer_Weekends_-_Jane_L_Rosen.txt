### Review:
**Plot and Structure:**
The story follows Addison, who inherits a beach house on Fire Island from her aunt, Gicky. As she navigates her new life, she meets Ben, a widowed author who is initially hesitant to open his heart to love again. The plot is engaging, and the romance between Addison and Ben is well-developed, with their chemistry and emotional connection standing out as highlights.

**Characters:**
Addison is a relatable protagonist, and her journey of self-discovery and growth is compelling. Ben, the widowed author, is a well-crafted character, and his emotional depth adds significant depth to the story. The supporting cast, including the quirky neighbors and friends, enriches the narrative and enhances the overall experience.

**Writing and Language:**
The author's writing is engaging and well-executed, making the story both enjoyable and easy to follow.

**World-Building and Setting:**
The beach house on Fire Island serves as a charming and idyllic backdrop for the story, enhancing the overall atmosphere and setting.

**Themes:**
The novel explores themes of self-discovery, love, and the importance of embracing new experiences. It also touches on grief and the journey of healing, adding depth to the narrative.

**Emotional Impact:**
The story is emotionally resonant, particularly in its portrayal of grief and the journey of healing. The romance between Addison and Ben is heartwarming and adds significant emotional depth to the narrative.

**Enjoyment and Engagement:**
The novel is a delightful summer read, offering a light yet engaging story that is perfect for relaxation.

**Expectation Fulfillment:**
The novel is a perfect blend of romance, self-discovery, and small-town charm, making it an ideal choice for readers seeking a heartwarming and engaging story.

### Overall Assessment:
Seven Summer Weekends is a heartwarming and engaging novel that combines romance, self-discovery, and small-town charm. With its relatable protagonist, well-developed characters, and emotional depth, it's a perfect summer read. The story is both light and meaningful, making it a delightful choice for readers seeking a feel-good novel.

### Score: 4.0

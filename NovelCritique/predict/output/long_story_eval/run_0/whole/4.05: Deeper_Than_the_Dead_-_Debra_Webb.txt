### Review:
**Plot and Structure:**
The story is engaging, with a well-paced narrative that keeps readers invested in Vera's journey. The plot is filled with twists and turns, making it challenging to predict the outcome.

**Characters:**
Vera Boyett is a compelling protagonist, and her journey from a disgraced deputy police chief to a determined investigator is both inspiring and relatable. The supporting characters, including Sheriff Bent and Pete Brooks, add depth to the narrative, while the antagonists, such as Judge Higdon and Florence, are well-developed and menacing.

**Writing and Language:**
The author's writing is engaging and accessible, making the story easy to follow and enjoy.

**World-Building and Setting:**
The small-town setting of Fayetteville, Tennessee, is vividly portrayed, immersing readers in the story's atmosphere.

**Themes:**
The novel explores themes of family, loyalty, and the pursuit of justice, adding depth and emotional resonance to the narrative.

**Emotional Impact:**
The story is emotionally charged, particularly as Vera confronts her past and the secrets that have haunted her family for years.

**Enjoyment and Engagement:**
This book is a captivating read, and I highly recommend it to fans of mystery and thriller genres.

**Expectation Fulfillment:**
The novel's premise is intriguing, and the author's experience as a crime analyst adds authenticity to the story.

### Overall Assessment:
Deeper Than the Dead is a gripping mystery novel with a well-crafted plot, relatable characters, and a small-town setting that comes alive. The emotional depth and twists keep readers engaged, making it a highly recommended read.

### Score: 4.5

### Review:
**Plot and Structure:**
The story was engaging, but the pacing felt uneven, with some sections dragging on while others moved too quickly. The plot twist involving the Minotaur's past was intriguing, but it felt rushed and underdeveloped.

**Characters:**
The characters were well-developed, and their relationships were compelling. However, the romance felt rushed, and the tension between the Minotaur and Ariadne was not fully explored.

**Writing and Language:**
The writing was engaging, and the author's use of Greek mythology added depth to the story.

**World-Building and Setting:**
The Greek mythology setting was well-executed, and the author's use of it added significant depth to the story.

**Themes:**
The story effectively explores themes of power, control, and the complexities of relationships.

**Emotional Impact:**
The story elicited strong emotions, particularly in the scenes involving the Minotaur and Ariadne.

**Enjoyment and Engagement:**
Overall, I enjoyed the story and found it engaging, but it fell short of being a 5-star read due to its flaws.

**Expectation Fulfillment:**
I was excited to read this book, as I've enjoyed the author's previous works. However, this one didn't quite meet my expectations.

### Overall Assessment:
Overall, I enjoyed the story and found it engaging, but it fell short of being a 5-star read due to its uneven pacing, rushed plot twists, and underdeveloped romance. Despite these flaws, the well-developed characters, Greek mythology setting, and emotional depth made it a worthwhile read.

### Score: 3.5

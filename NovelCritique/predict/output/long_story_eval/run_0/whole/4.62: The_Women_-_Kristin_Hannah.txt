### Review:
**Plot and Structure:**
The story follows Frankie McGrath, a young woman who joins the Army Nurse Corps in 1965 to serve in Vietnam. The narrative explores her journey, from the chaos of war to her struggles with PTSD and addiction upon returning home. The plot is engaging, and the author skillfully weaves together themes of friendship, patriotism, and personal growth.

**Characters:**
Frankie McGrath is a well-developed protagonist whose journey from idealism to trauma and recovery is compelling. The supporting characters, including Barb, Ethel, and Henry, enrich the narrative with their own struggles and growth. The author's portrayal of Frankie's relationships, particularly with Rye and Henry, adds depth to her character.

**Writing and Language:**
The author's writing is engaging and immersive, making it easy to become fully invested in Frankie's story. The vivid descriptions of the war and its aftermath are particularly effective in conveying the chaos and destruction.

**World-Building and Setting:**
The author skillfully transports readers to the Vietnam War era, immersing them in the chaos and destruction of the conflict. The vivid descriptions of the war and its aftermath are particularly effective in conveying the intensity of the experience.

**Themes:**
The novel explores themes of idealism, trauma, and recovery, highlighting the sacrifices made by women during the Vietnam War. It also delves into the challenges of returning home and the struggle to reintegrate into society.

**Emotional Impact:**
The story is emotionally resonant, particularly in its portrayal of Frankie's struggles with PTSD and addiction. The author's vivid descriptions of the war and its aftermath evoke a strong sense of empathy and understanding.

**Enjoyment and Engagement:**
The novel is a captivating read, with a well-paced narrative that keeps readers engaged from start to finish. The author's ability to balance action, emotion, and character development makes the story both enjoyable and memorable.

**Expectation Fulfillment:**
The novel is a compelling historical fiction that offers a fresh perspective on the Vietnam War. It highlights the often-overlooked contributions of women during this period, making it a valuable addition to the genre.

### Overall Assessment:
The Women is a compelling historical fiction novel that offers a fresh perspective on the Vietnam War through the eyes of a young woman. With its well-developed characters, vivid descriptions, and emotionally resonant themes, the story is both engaging and memorable. The author's ability to balance action, emotion, and character development makes this a standout read.

### Score: 4.5

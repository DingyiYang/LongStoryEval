### Review:
**Plot and Structure:**
The story follows a series of murders targeting teenagers, with the killer using a small, concealed weapon. The investigation is led by Lieutenant Eve Dallas, who must uncover the motives behind these heinous crimes.

**Characters:**
The protagonist, Lieutenant Eve Dallas, is a seasoned investigator who leads the investigation into the murders. Her determination and expertise are essential in uncovering the killer's identity and motives.

**Writing and Language:**
The author's writing style is engaging and well-suited for the genre, making the story both enjoyable and accessible.

**World-Building and Setting:**
The story is set in New York, providing a vivid backdrop for the investigation and adding depth to the narrative.

**Themes:**
The novel explores themes of murder, investigation, and the complexities of human behavior, particularly in the context of privilege and entitlement.

**Emotional Impact:**
The story is emotionally gripping, particularly as the investigation unfolds and the motives behind the murders are revealed.

**Enjoyment and Engagement:**
The novel is a compelling read, with a well-crafted plot and engaging characters that keep readers invested in the story.

**Expectation Fulfillment:**
The novel delivers on its promise of a gripping murder mystery, with a well-executed investigation and a satisfying resolution.

### Overall Assessment:
Random in Death is a gripping murder mystery that delivers on its promise of a thrilling investigation. With a well-crafted plot, engaging characters, and a satisfying resolution, it's a must-read for fans of the genre.

### Score: 4.5

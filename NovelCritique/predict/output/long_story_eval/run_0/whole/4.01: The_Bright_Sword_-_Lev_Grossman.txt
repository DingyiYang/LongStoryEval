### Review:
**Plot and Structure:**
The story follows Collum, a young knight, as he joins a group of misfit knights to rebuild Camelot after King Arthur's death. The narrative is rich with historical and mythological references, making it both engaging and informative.

**Characters:**
The characters are well-developed and intriguing, each with their own unique personalities and motivations. Collum's journey from a young knight to a leader is particularly compelling.

**Writing and Language:**
The author's writing is engaging and immersive, making it easy to become fully invested in the story.

**World-Building and Setting:**
The novel offers a fresh perspective on the Arthurian legend, focusing on the misfit knights who survived Camelot's downfall. This unique approach adds depth and intrigue to the narrative.

**Themes:**
The novel explores themes of identity, leadership, and the complexities of rebuilding a kingdom. It also delves into the consequences of Arthur's death and the impact on the surviving knights.

**Emotional Impact:**
The story evokes a range of emotions, from excitement and curiosity to sadness and reflection. The characters' struggles and triumphs are deeply moving.

**Enjoyment and Engagement:**
The novel is a captivating read, with a perfect blend of action, adventure, and emotional depth. It kept me engaged from start to finish.

**Expectation Fulfillment:**
The novel is a retelling of the Arthurian legend, but it offers a fresh and unique perspective. It's perfect for fans of the original story and those looking for a compelling fantasy adventure.

### Overall Assessment:
The Bright Sword is a captivating retelling of the Arthurian legend, offering a fresh perspective on the misfit knights who survived Camelot's downfall. With well-developed characters, a richly detailed world, and a compelling plot, this novel is a must-read for fans of fantasy and historical fiction.

### Score: 4.5

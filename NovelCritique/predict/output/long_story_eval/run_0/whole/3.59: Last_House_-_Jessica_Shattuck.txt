### Review:
**Plot and Structure:**
The story follows the Taylor family across multiple generations, spanning nearly 80 years. It begins with Nick Taylor, a WWII veteran and lawyer, and his wife, Bet, a former codebreaker turned homemaker. Their children, Katherine and Harry, navigate the challenges of growing up during the 1960s, while Katherine's activism and Harry's environmentalism lead to significant family conflicts. The narrative shifts to 2026, where Katherine, now a mother, grapples with the legacy of her brother's radical activism and the consequences of her own choices.

**Characters:**
The characters are well-developed and relatable, with each generation facing unique challenges and struggles. The family's dynamics are particularly compelling, as they navigate their differences and confront the consequences of their choices.

**Writing and Language:**
The author's writing is engaging and accessible, making the story easy to follow and enjoy.

**World-Building and Setting:**
The story is set against the backdrop of significant historical events, including the Vietnam War, the Civil Rights Movement, and the rise of environmental activism. These events are skillfully woven into the narrative, enriching the characters' experiences and the overall context.

**Themes:**
The novel explores themes of family, identity, activism, and the consequences of choice. It delves into the complexities of growing up during times of social and political upheaval, highlighting the challenges of reconciling personal beliefs with family expectations.

**Emotional Impact:**
The story is emotionally resonant, particularly in its portrayal of family conflicts and the struggles of growing up during tumultuous times. The characters' journeys are both heartwarming and heartbreaking, making the narrative deeply engaging.

**Enjoyment and Engagement:**
The novel is a captivating read, with a well-paced narrative that keeps readers engaged from start to finish.

**Expectation Fulfillment:**
The novel's premise is intriguing, and the author's ability to weave historical events into the narrative is impressive.

### Overall Assessment:
Last House is a compelling family saga that explores the complexities of growing up during times of social and political upheaval. With well-developed characters, a rich historical context, and a gripping narrative, the novel is a must-read for fans of historical fiction and family dramas.

### Score: 4.5

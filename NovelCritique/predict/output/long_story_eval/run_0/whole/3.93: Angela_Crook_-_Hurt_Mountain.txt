### Review:
**Plot and Structure:**
The story follows Olivia and Brandon, who are reunited to investigate the disappearance of their daughter, Carly, after a traumatic event involving a young girl named Delilah. The plot is engaging, with a well-paced narrative that keeps readers invested in the characters' journey.

**Characters:**
Olivia and Brandon are well-developed characters, each grappling with their own grief and trauma. Their journey of healing and reconciliation is both poignant and compelling. Delilah, the young girl at the center of the mystery, is a standout character, and her relationship with Olivia adds depth to the story.

**Writing and Language:**
The author's writing is engaging and accessible, making the story easy to follow and enjoy.

**World-Building and Setting:**
The Colorado setting is vividly described, adding depth and atmosphere to the story.

**Themes:**
The novel explores themes of grief, trauma, and healing, offering a poignant and realistic portrayal of the emotional journey of its characters.

**Emotional Impact:**
The story is emotionally resonant, particularly in its portrayal of grief and trauma. The characters' struggles and triumphs are deeply moving, making the narrative both heartbreaking and uplifting.

**Enjoyment and Engagement:**
The story is engaging and well-paced, keeping readers invested in the characters' journey.

**Expectation Fulfillment:**
The novel's premise is intriguing, and the author delivers on its promise, crafting a compelling and emotionally resonant story.

### Overall Assessment:
Hurt Mountain is a well-crafted novel that combines an intriguing premise with emotionally resonant characters and a gripping plot. The author's writing is accessible and engaging, making the story a compelling read. While the ending may not be entirely satisfying, the journey of healing and reconciliation is deeply moving and worth the investment.

### Score: 4.0

### Review:
**Plot and Structure:**
The story's pacing was uneven, with some sections feeling rushed and others dragging on. The plot was engaging, but the lack of a clear resolution left me wanting more.

**Characters:**
Tirne's journey of self-discovery was compelling, and her relationships with Autumn and Sidriel added depth to the narrative. However, the absence of a clear resolution left me feeling unsatisfied.

**Writing and Language:**
The writing was beautiful and immersive, with vivid descriptions that brought the world to life. However, the pacing issues and lack of resolution detracted from the overall experience.

**World-Building and Setting:**
The world-building was exceptional, with a richly detailed and immersive setting that drew me in from the start.

**Themes:**
The story's exploration of identity, morality, and the blurred lines between good and evil was thought-provoking and well-executed.

**Emotional Impact:**
The story elicited strong emotions, particularly in the moments between Tirne and Autumn. However, the lack of a clear resolution left me feeling unsatisfied.

**Enjoyment and Engagement:**
I thoroughly enjoyed the story and was captivated by the world and characters. However, the uneven pacing and lack of resolution left me wanting more.

**Expectation Fulfillment:**
I was initially hesitant due to the fantasy genre, but the story's focus on character development and emotional depth made it a compelling read.

### Overall Assessment:
Overall, I thoroughly enjoyed the story's world-building, character development, and emotional depth. However, the uneven pacing and lack of a clear resolution left me wanting more. Despite these issues, the story's beauty and thought-provoking themes made it a memorable read.

### Score: 3.5

### Review:
**Plot and Structure:**
The story's pacing was inconsistent, with some sections feeling rushed and others dragging on. The plot twist involving the hurricane was particularly underwhelming, as it failed to deliver the expected impact.

**Characters:**
The characters were well-developed, and their relationships added depth to the story. However, the plot twist involving the hurricane felt forced and lacked the emotional weight it deserved.

**Writing and Language:**
The writing was engaging, and the author's use of vivid imagery and metaphors added depth to the narrative.

**World-Building and Setting:**
The setting of Puerto Rico was vividly portrayed, and the author's use of local slang and cultural references added authenticity to the story.

**Themes:**
The novel explores themes of grief, revenge, and the supernatural, which are compelling and well-executed.

**Emotional Impact:**
The story's emotional impact was diminished by the underwhelming plot twist involving the hurricane.

**Enjoyment and Engagement:**
The novel was engaging, but the inconsistent pacing and underwhelming plot twist left me feeling disappointed.

**Expectation Fulfillment:**
The novel's premise was intriguing, but the execution fell short of my expectations.

### Overall Assessment:
While the novel had a strong premise, well-developed characters, and vivid writing, the inconsistent pacing and underwhelming plot twist involving the hurricane left me feeling disappointed. Despite these flaws, the story's emotional depth and cultural authenticity made it a worthwhile read.

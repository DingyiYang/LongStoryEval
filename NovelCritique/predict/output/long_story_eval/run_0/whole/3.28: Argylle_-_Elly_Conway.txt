### Review:
**Plot and Structure:**
The story follows Frances Coffey, a CIA spymaster, and her team as they navigate a mission to uncover the Amber Room, a legendary treasure. The plot is engaging, filled with twists and turns that keep readers guessing until the very end.

**Characters:**
The characters are well-developed and complex, with Frances Coffey standing out as a particularly compelling figure. Her leadership and strategic thinking are impressive, and her relationship with her team adds depth to the narrative.

**Writing and Language:**
The author's writing is engaging and immersive, making it easy to become fully invested in the story.

**World-Building and Setting:**
The story's setting is vividly described, immersing readers in the world of espionage and international intrigue.

**Themes:**
The novel explores themes of loyalty, duty, and the complexities of leadership, adding depth to the narrative.

**Emotional Impact:**
The story is emotionally resonant, particularly in its portrayal of Frances Coffey's leadership and her team's struggles.

**Enjoyment and Engagement:**
The novel is a thrilling and engaging read, with a plot that keeps readers hooked from start to finish.

**Expectation Fulfillment:**
The novel delivers on its promise of a thrilling and action-packed story, meeting and exceeding expectations.

### Overall Assessment:
Argylle is a gripping and well-crafted novel that combines thrilling action, complex characters, and emotionally resonant themes. The author's writing is immersive, and the story is filled with twists and turns that keep readers engaged until the very end. I highly recommend this book to fans of espionage thrillers and international intrigue.

### Score: 4.5

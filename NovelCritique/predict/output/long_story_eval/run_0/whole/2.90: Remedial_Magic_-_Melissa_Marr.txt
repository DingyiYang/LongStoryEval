### Review:
**Plot and Structure:**
The story's pacing was uneven, with some sections feeling rushed and others dragging on. The plot was overly complex, featuring multiple characters and subplots that made it difficult to follow. The ending was particularly disappointing, as it felt rushed and left many questions unanswered.

**Characters:**
The characters were well-developed, but their motivations and actions often felt inconsistent. The romance between Ellie and Prospero was underdeveloped and lacked chemistry, making it difficult to connect with their relationship.

**Writing and Language:**
The writing style was engaging, and the author's use of language was a standout feature of the book.

**World-Building and Setting:**
The magical world of Crenshaw was richly detailed and immersive, making it a standout aspect of the book.

**Themes:**
The book's themes of power, control, and manipulation were compelling, but the execution fell short due to the complexity of the plot and the lack of clear resolution.

**Emotional Impact:**
The book had the potential to be emotionally impactful, but the rushed ending and lack of resolution left me feeling frustrated and unsatisfied.

**Enjoyment and Engagement:**
I was initially excited to read this book, but the uneven pacing and lack of resolution left me feeling disappointed and frustrated.

**Expectation Fulfillment:**
I was excited to read this book, but it ultimately fell short of my expectations.

### Overall Assessment:
While the book had a strong start with its engaging writing style and richly detailed magical world, it ultimately fell short due to its uneven pacing, complex plot, and lack of resolution. The romance and character development were underwhelming, and the ending left me feeling frustrated. Despite these flaws, the book had potential and could have been a great read with more focus on character development and plot clarity.

### Score: 2.5

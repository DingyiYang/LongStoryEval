### Review:
**Plot and Structure:**
The story follows Emily, a writer and barista, who enters a fake relationship with Mark, an engineer, to appease her mother's matchmaking efforts. As they spend more time together, their initial dislike for each other gradually turns into a genuine connection. The plot is engaging and well-paced, making it easy to follow and enjoy.

**Characters:**
Emily and Mark are well-developed characters, each with their own flaws and strengths. Their initial dislike for each other adds depth to their relationship, making their eventual connection more believable and satisfying. The supporting characters, including Emily's mother and sisters, are also well-crafted, adding to the story's charm.

**Writing and Language:**
The writing is engaging and easy to follow, making the story enjoyable from start to finish.

**World-Building and Setting:**
The story is set in Toronto, which adds a unique and refreshing backdrop to the narrative.

**Themes:**
The story explores themes of family, love, and self-discovery, making it both heartwarming and relatable.

**Emotional Impact:**
The story is emotionally engaging, with a perfect balance of humor and heartwarming moments.

**Enjoyment and Engagement:**
This book is a delightful read, filled with humor, heart, and a touch of spice. It's perfect for anyone looking for a lighthearted yet engaging romance.

**Expectation Fulfillment:**
This book is a perfect blend of romance, humor, and heartwarming moments, making it a must-read for fans of the genre.

### Overall Assessment:
This book is a delightful and engaging romance that combines humor, heartwarming moments, and a touch of spice. With well-developed characters, a unique setting, and a perfect balance of emotions, it's a must-read for fans of the genre.

### Score: 4.5

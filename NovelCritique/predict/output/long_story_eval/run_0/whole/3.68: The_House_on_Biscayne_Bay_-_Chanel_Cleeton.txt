### Review:
**Plot and Structure:**
The story's pacing was uneven, with some sections feeling rushed and others dragging on. The mystery was intriguing, but the resolution felt rushed and lacked sufficient buildup.

**Characters:**
The characters were well-developed, and their relationships added depth to the story. However, the lack of a clear villain made it difficult to fully invest in the mystery.

**Writing and Language:**
The writing was engaging, and the author's use of vivid descriptions effectively brought the setting to life.

**World-Building and Setting:**
The setting was beautifully described, and the author's use of vivid imagery made the Miami of the 1920s come alive.

**Themes:**
The novel explores themes of family, love, and the complexities of human relationships, which are central to the story.

**Emotional Impact:**
The story elicited a range of emotions, from excitement to sadness, and kept me engaged throughout.

**Enjoyment and Engagement:**
I thoroughly enjoyed the novel and found it to be a captivating read.

**Expectation Fulfillment:**
The novel's blend of historical fiction, mystery, and gothic elements made for an intriguing and unique reading experience.

### Overall Assessment:
Overall, I thoroughly enjoyed this novel, despite some minor flaws in pacing and character development. The author's vivid descriptions and engaging writing style made the story come alive, and the exploration of family and love added depth to the narrative. While the mystery resolution felt rushed, the emotional impact and unique blend of genres made this a memorable read.

### Score: 4.0

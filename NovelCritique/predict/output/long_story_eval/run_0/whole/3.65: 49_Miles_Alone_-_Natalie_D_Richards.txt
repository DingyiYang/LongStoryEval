### Review:
**Plot and Structure:**
The story follows Katie and Aster as they embark on a four-day hike in the Utah desert, only to encounter a young couple, Riley and Finn, whose disappearance sets them on a quest to uncover the truth. The plot is engaging, with a well-paced narrative that keeps me invested in the characters' journey.

**Characters:**
Katie and Aster are well-developed characters, each grappling with their own challenges. Katie's journey of healing and growth is particularly compelling, as she confronts her past and learns to trust herself. The supporting characters, including Riley and Finn, add depth to the story, though their motivations and actions can be somewhat confusing at times.

**Writing and Language:**
The writing is engaging and well-paced, making the story easy to follow and enjoy.

**World-Building and Setting:**
The Utah desert serves as a vivid and immersive backdrop, enhancing the story's tension and emotional depth.

**Themes:**
The novel explores themes of healing, trust, and resilience, particularly through Katie's journey. It also delves into the complexities of relationships and the challenges of navigating uncertainty.

**Emotional Impact:**
The story is emotionally resonant, particularly in its portrayal of Katie's healing journey and the tension surrounding Riley and Finn's disappearance.

**Enjoyment and Engagement:**
The novel is a quick and engaging read, making it perfect for a summer day.

**Expectation Fulfillment:**
The novel's premise is intriguing, and the author's background in wilderness therapy adds a unique and authentic touch to the story.

### Overall Assessment:
Overall, this is a well-written and emotionally resonant novel that explores themes of healing, trust, and resilience. While the plot and characters are engaging, some aspects of the supporting characters' motivations and actions could be clearer. The Utah desert setting and Katie's journey make the story a compelling and enjoyable read.

### Score: 3.5

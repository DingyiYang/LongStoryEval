### Review:
**Plot and Structure:**
The story's pacing was inconsistent, with some sections feeling rushed and others dragging on. The plot twist involving King Kitt's decision to marry Paedyn was both unexpected and intriguing, but it left me wanting more resolution.

**Characters:**
Paedyn's character development was impressive, and her journey from vengeance to self-discovery was compelling. However, Kai's transformation felt rushed, and his internal conflict lacked depth. The romance between Paedyn and Kai was sweet but underdeveloped, leaving me wanting more emotional connection between them.

**Writing and Language:**
The writing was engaging, and the author's use of metaphors and imagery added depth to the story.

**World-Building and Setting:**
The world-building was impressive, with a richly detailed setting that added depth to the story.

**Themes:**
The story effectively explores themes of rebellion, power, and the complexities of loyalty and duty.

**Emotional Impact:**
The story elicited strong emotions, particularly in the scenes where Paedyn and Kai were separated, which added to the overall tension.

**Enjoyment and Engagement:**
The story was engaging, and I found myself invested in the characters and their journeys.

**Expectation Fulfillment:**
The book's premise was intriguing, and I was excited to dive into the story.

### Overall Assessment:
Overall, I enjoyed the book and found it to be a solid read. While the pacing and character development were inconsistent, the world-building, emotional impact, and intriguing plot twists made it a worthwhile experience. I'm excited to see where the story goes next.

### Score: 3.5

### Review:
**Plot and Structure:**
The story's pacing was uneven, with some sections feeling rushed and others dragging on. The plot twist involving the fire was predictable, and the resolution of the mystery lacked depth.

**Characters:**
The characters were well-developed, but their motivations and actions often felt inconsistent. The protagonist's obsession with Francesca was excessive, and her relationship with her brother was underdeveloped.

**Writing and Language:**
The writing was engaging, and the author's use of descriptive language effectively set the scene. However, the excessive use of adjectives and adverbs detracted from the overall flow.

**World-Building and Setting:**
The setting was vividly described, and the author's use of sensory details effectively immersed me in the world.

**Themes:**
The story's exploration of trauma and its impact on individuals and relationships was compelling. However, the themes were not fully developed, leaving some aspects feeling underexplored.

**Emotional Impact:**
The story elicited strong emotions, particularly in the scenes involving Francesca's past and her relationship with her brother. However, the emotional impact was diminished by the predictable plot twist and the lack of depth in the resolution.

**Enjoyment and Engagement:**
I found the story engaging, but the uneven pacing and predictable plot twist detracted from my overall enjoyment.

**Expectation Fulfillment:**
I was initially drawn to the book's premise, but the execution fell short of my expectations.

### Overall Assessment:
While the story had its strengths, particularly in its vivid setting and emotional depth, it was ultimately let down by uneven pacing, predictable plot twists, and underdeveloped themes. Despite these flaws, I found the story engaging enough to finish, and I'm curious to see how the author's future works will improve.

### Score: 3.2

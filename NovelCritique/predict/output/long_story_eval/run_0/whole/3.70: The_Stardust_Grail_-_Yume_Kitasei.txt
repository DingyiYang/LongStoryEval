### Review:
**Plot and Structure:**
The story's pacing was uneven, with some sections feeling rushed and others dragging on. The plot was engaging, but the lack of a clear resolution left me feeling unsatisfied.

**Characters:**
The characters were well-developed, and their interactions were engaging. However, the absence of a clear resolution left me feeling disconnected from their journeys.

**Writing and Language:**
The writing was engaging, and the author's use of language was impressive. However, the lack of a clear resolution left me feeling unsatisfied.

**World-Building and Setting:**
The world-building was impressive, with a richly detailed universe that captivated me. The setting was immersive, and I found myself fully engaged in the story.

**Themes:**
The story's exploration of the consequences of saving one species while potentially condemning another was thought-provoking and added depth to the narrative.

**Emotional Impact:**
The story's emotional impact was significant, particularly in its exploration of Maya's visions and the crew's struggles. However, the lack of a clear resolution left me feeling unsatisfied.

**Enjoyment and Engagement:**
I thoroughly enjoyed the story and found myself fully engaged in the narrative. However, the uneven pacing and lack of a clear resolution left me feeling unsatisfied.

**Expectation Fulfillment:**
I was excited to read this book, and it did not disappoint. The premise was intriguing, and the story delivered on its promise.

### Overall Assessment:
Overall, I thoroughly enjoyed this book and found the premise, world-building, and characters to be exceptional. However, the uneven pacing and lack of a clear resolution left me feeling unsatisfied. Despite this, I would still recommend the book to others, as it is a well-written and engaging story.

### Score: 3.5

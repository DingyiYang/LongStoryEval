### Review:
**Plot and Structure:**
The story follows Meghna, a theater teacher, and Karthik, an engineer, as they pretend to be engaged to avoid their families' matchmaking efforts. Their fake relationship blossoms into real feelings, but they struggle to reconcile their desires with societal expectations.

**Characters:**
Meghna and Karthik are well-developed characters, each with their own struggles and aspirations. Their journey from pretending to be in love to genuinely falling for each other is both heartwarming and relatable.

**Writing and Language:**
The author's writing is engaging and easy to follow, making the story enjoyable from start to finish.

**World-Building and Setting:**
The Indian-American setting enriches the story, adding depth and authenticity to the characters' experiences.

**Themes:**
The novel explores themes of love, family expectations, and personal growth, making it a compelling read.

**Emotional Impact:**
The story is emotionally engaging, with moments of humor and heartwarming romance that kept me invested in the characters' journey.

**Enjoyment and Engagement:**
I thoroughly enjoyed this book and found it hard to put down. The engaging plot and well-developed characters made it a delightful read.

**Expectation Fulfillment:**
This book is perfect for fans of fake-relationship romances and Indian-American culture. It's a heartwarming and engaging read that I highly recommend.

### Overall Assessment:
This novel is a delightful blend of romance, family dynamics, and personal growth, set against the vibrant backdrop of Indian-American culture. With well-developed characters, an engaging plot, and a touch of humor, it's a story that will leave you smiling and rooting for the couple.

### Score: 4.5

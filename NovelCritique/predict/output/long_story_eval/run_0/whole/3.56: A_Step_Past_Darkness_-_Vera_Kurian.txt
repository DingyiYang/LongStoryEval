### Review:
**Plot and Structure:**
The story follows six high school students who witness a horrific crime in an abandoned coal mine, leading them to form a pact of silence. Twenty years later, they are reunited to uncover the truth behind Maddy's murder. The plot is engaging, and the twist of the group's reunion adds depth to the narrative.

**Characters:**
The characters are well-developed and relatable, each with distinct personalities and motivations. Their growth and evolution throughout the story are compelling, making their journey both enjoyable and meaningful.

**Writing and Language:**
The writing is engaging and well-paced, making the story easy to follow and enjoy.

**World-Building and Setting:**
The setting of Wesley Falls, a small town with a dark history, is vividly portrayed, adding depth and intrigue to the story.

**Themes:**
The novel explores themes of friendship, loyalty, and the power of truth, which are central to the narrative.

**Emotional Impact:**
The story evokes a range of emotions, from suspense and intrigue to joy and satisfaction, as the characters navigate their journey.

**Enjoyment and Engagement:**
The story is highly engaging, and the twist of the group's reunion adds a layer of excitement and intrigue.

**Expectation Fulfillment:**
The novel's premise is intriguing, and the execution meets my expectations.

### Overall Assessment:
A Step Past Darkness is a gripping and emotionally resonant novel that combines suspense, friendship, and loyalty. The well-developed characters, engaging plot, and vivid setting make it a highly enjoyable read. I would recommend this book to fans of mystery and thriller genres.

### Score: 4.5

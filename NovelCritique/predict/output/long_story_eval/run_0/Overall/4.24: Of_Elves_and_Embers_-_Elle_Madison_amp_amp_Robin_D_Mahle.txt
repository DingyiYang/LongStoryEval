### Review:
**Plot and Structure:**
The story follows Ember, a woman who travels to Vegas for a girls' weekend but finds herself transported to a fantasy world. There, she is captured by King Hadeon, who believes she is the reincarnation of his arch-nemesis. The plot is engaging, with Ember's journey to uncover her past life and unite the courts adding depth to the narrative.

**Characters:**
Ember's journey from a reluctant participant to a determined leader is compelling. Her relationship with Hadeon, who initially views her as an enemy, evolves into a complex and intriguing dynamic. The supporting characters, including Ember's brothers and allies, add depth to the story.

**Writing and Language:**
The writing is engaging, and the pacing keeps me hooked from start to finish.

**World-Building and Setting:**
The fantasy world is richly detailed, with a unique magic system and a compelling history that underpins the story.

**Themes:**
The novel explores themes of identity, belonging, and the complexities of relationships, particularly in the context of a fantasy world.

**Emotional Impact:**
The story is emotionally resonant, particularly in its portrayal of Ember's journey and her evolving relationship with Hadeon.

**Enjoyment and Engagement:**
I thoroughly enjoyed this novel and found it hard to put down. The engaging plot, well-developed characters, and emotional depth made it a memorable read.

**Expectation Fulfillment:**
This novel is a perfect blend of fantasy and romance, offering a unique and captivating experience.

### Overall Assessment:
Of Elves & Embers is a captivating fantasy romance that combines a unique magic system, rich world-building, and emotionally resonant characters. The story follows Ember's journey from a reluctant participant to a determined leader, with a complex and evolving relationship at its core. The novel is a perfect blend of fantasy and romance, making it a memorable and enjoyable read.

### Score: 4.5

### Review:
**Plot and Structure:**
The story's pacing was uneven, with some sections feeling rushed and others dragging on. The mystery was intriguing, but the resolution felt rushed and lacked the depth I had hoped for.

**Characters:**
The characters were well-developed, and I appreciated the exploration of their relationships and personal growth. However, the romance subplot felt underdeveloped and lacked the depth I had anticipated.

**Writing and Language:**
The writing was engaging, and the author's use of metaphors and vivid descriptions added depth to the narrative.

**World-Building and Setting:**
The setting of Ralston Island was beautifully crafted, and the historical elements added a unique and intriguing layer to the story.

**Themes:**
The novel explores themes of family, love, and the complexities of human relationships, which I found both compelling and thought-provoking.

**Emotional Impact:**
The story elicited a range of emotions, from excitement to sadness, and I appreciated the depth of the characters' experiences.

**Enjoyment and Engagement:**
I thoroughly enjoyed the story and found it to be a captivating read. The author's writing style and the unique setting kept me engaged from start to finish.

**Expectation Fulfillment:**
I was initially drawn to the book by its intriguing premise and the promise of a mystery set in a beautiful, isolated location. While the story delivered on these expectations, it fell short in some areas, particularly in the romance subplot and the resolution of the mystery.

### Overall Assessment:
Overall, I enjoyed the novel for its unique setting, well-developed characters, and exploration of complex themes. However, the uneven pacing, underdeveloped romance subplot, and rushed resolution of the mystery left me wanting more. Despite these flaws, the story was engaging and thought-provoking, making it a worthwhile read.

### Score: 3.5

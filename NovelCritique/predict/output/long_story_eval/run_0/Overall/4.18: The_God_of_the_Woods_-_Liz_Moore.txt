### Review:
**Plot and Structure:**
The novel's pacing is uneven, with some sections feeling slow and others rushed. The mystery surrounding Barbara's disappearance is intriguing, but the resolution feels rushed and lacks the depth I had hoped for.

**Characters:**
The characters are well-developed, and their relationships are intricately woven throughout the story. However, the resolution of Barbara's disappearance feels rushed and lacks the depth I had hoped for.

**Writing and Language:**
The writing is beautiful and evocative, capturing the essence of the 1970s summer camp setting. The author's vivid descriptions of the woods and the characters' experiences are truly immersive.

**World-Building and Setting:**
The 1970s summer camp setting is vividly portrayed, with the author's descriptions of the woods and the characters' experiences creating a strong sense of immersion.

**Themes:**
The novel explores themes of family, power, and the consequences of secrets, which are thought-provoking and well-executed.

**Emotional Impact:**
The novel's emotional depth is impressive, particularly in its portrayal of the characters' relationships and the consequences of their actions.

**Enjoyment and Engagement:**
The novel is a compelling read, and I found myself thoroughly engaged in the story.

**Expectation Fulfillment:**
I was initially drawn to the novel's premise, which promised a thrilling mystery set in a 1970s summer camp. While the story delivered on its promise, it ultimately fell short of my expectations.

### Overall Assessment:
While the novel's premise and setting were captivating, the uneven pacing and rushed resolution of the mystery left me wanting more. The beautiful writing and well-developed characters were highlights, but the story's potential was not fully realized.

### Score: 3.5

### Review:
**Plot and Structure:**
The story follows Dr. Lionel Scott, a renowned scientist who believes humanity is the primary threat to the planet. His disappearance prompts U.S. Marshal Lucas Davenport and his daughter, Letty, to investigate. As they delve deeper, they uncover a sinister plot involving a deadly virus that could endanger the world.

**Characters:**
The characters are well-developed, and their interactions are engaging. The dynamic between Lucas and Letty adds depth to the story, while the villain, Dr. Scott, is both intriguing and terrifying.                                                                        

**Writing and Language:**
The author's writing is engaging and easy to follow, making the story enjoyable from start to finish.

**World-Building and Setting:**
The story is set in New Mexico, which adds a unique and immersive backdrop to the narrative.

**Themes:**
The novel explores the theme of humanity's destructive nature and the consequences of unchecked scientific advancements.

**Emotional Impact:**
The story is both thrilling and emotionally resonant, keeping me engaged from start to finish.

**Enjoyment and Engagement:**
I thoroughly enjoyed this novel and found it to be a compelling read.

**Expectation Fulfillment:**
This novel is a thrilling mystery that combines elements of science and suspense.

### Overall Assessment:
Toxic Prey is a gripping and well-crafted thriller that combines science, suspense, and a unique setting. The characters are engaging, and the story is both thrilling and emotionally resonant. I highly recommend this novel to fans of the genre.                         

### Score: 4.5

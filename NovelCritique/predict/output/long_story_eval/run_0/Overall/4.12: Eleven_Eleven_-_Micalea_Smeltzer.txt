### Review:
**Plot and Structure:**
The story follows Via, a divorced attorney who relocates to Maine to pursue her passion for art. She meets Reid, a 21-year-old local, and their relationship blossoms despite their age difference. The narrative explores Via's journey of self-discovery and her decision to open her heart to love again.

**Characters:**
Via's journey of self-discovery and her decision to open her heart to love again are compelling. However, the age gap between Via and Reid feels unrealistic and detracts from the story's believability.

**Writing and Language:**
The writing is engaging, and the story is well-paced, making it an enjoyable read.

**World-Building and Setting:**
The small-town setting of Maine adds charm to the story, enhancing its overall appeal.

**Themes:**
The story explores themes of self-discovery, love, and the importance of embracing change.

**Emotional Impact:**
The story is emotionally engaging, particularly as Via navigates her journey of self-discovery and love.

**Enjoyment and Engagement:**
The story is engaging and enjoyable, with a well-paced narrative that keeps readers invested.

**Expectation Fulfillment:**
The story's premise is intriguing, but the age gap between Via and Reid feels unrealistic and detracts from the narrative.

### Overall Assessment:
Overall, this is a sweet and emotionally engaging story about self-discovery and love. While the age gap between the main characters feels unrealistic, the small-town setting and Via's journey make it a worthwhile read.

### Score: 3.5

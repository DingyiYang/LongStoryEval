### Review:
**Plot and Structure:**
The story's pacing was excellent, and the plot was engaging, making it easy to stay invested in the characters' journeys.

**Characters:**
Frank's character development was exceptional, as he transitioned from a reclusive curmudgeon to a more open and empathetic individual. Red's determination and kindness also made him a compelling character. The relationships between Frank, Red, and the other characters were well-developed and added depth to the narrative.

**Writing and Language:**
The author's writing style was both engaging and accessible, making the story enjoyable to read.

**World-Building and Setting:**
The London setting was vividly portrayed, enhancing the story's atmosphere and depth.

**Themes:**
The story beautifully explores themes of grief, friendship, and the power of kindness, all of which are handled with sensitivity and depth.

**Emotional Impact:**
The story elicited a range of emotions, from laughter to tears, and left a lasting impression on me.

**Enjoyment and Engagement:**
I thoroughly enjoyed this book and found it to be a delightful read.

**Expectation Fulfillment:**
I was pleasantly surprised by how much I enjoyed this book, as I had initially thought it might not be my cup of tea.

### Overall Assessment:
This book was a delightful surprise, offering a heartwarming story with well-developed characters, a vivid setting, and a perfect balance of humor and emotion. It's a must-read for anyone looking for a touching and engaging tale.

### Score: 4.5

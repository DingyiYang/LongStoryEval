### Review:
**Plot and Structure:**
The novel's concept is intriguing, but the execution falls short. The story feels overly long, with too much repetition and unnecessary details that fail to enhance the plot. The pacing is slow, and the repetitive nature of the narrative makes it difficult to stay engaged.

**Characters:**
Marsh's character development is underwhelming, and her journey lacks depth. The repetitive nature of her experiences makes her growth feel stagnant. The supporting characters, including Ren, Dylan, and Harper, are underdeveloped and lack significant impact on the story.

**Writing and Language:**
The writing style is overly descriptive, which detracts from the story's overall flow. The repetitive nature of the narrative makes it feel like a chore to read.

**World-Building and Setting:**
The world-building is minimal, and the concept of the Bubble is underdeveloped. The technology behind it is vague, and the lack of detail makes it difficult to fully immerse oneself in the story.

**Themes:**
The novel's exploration of choice and its consequences is intriguing, but it is not executed effectively. The repetitive nature of the narrative makes it difficult to fully grasp the themes.

**Emotional Impact:**
The novel fails to evoke any significant emotions, as the repetitive nature of the story and the lack of character development make it difficult to connect with the characters or their experiences.

**Enjoyment and Engagement:**
I struggled to stay engaged with the novel due to its repetitive nature and slow pacing. The lack of character development and underdeveloped world-building made it difficult to find interest in the story.

**Expectation Fulfillment:**
I was initially excited about the novel's concept, but it failed to deliver. The execution was lacking, and the repetitive nature of the story made it a chore to read.

### Overall Assessment:
Overall, this novel was a disappointment. Despite its intriguing concept, the repetitive nature of the story, lack of character development, and underdeveloped world-building made it a chore to read. The slow pacing and lack of emotional impact further detracted from the experience. I would not recommend this book to others.

### Score: 1.5

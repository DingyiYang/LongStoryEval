### Review:
**Plot and Structure:**
The story's pacing was inconsistent, with some sections feeling rushed and others dragging on. The plot twist involving the time machine was predictable and lacked the surprise I had hoped for.

**Characters:**
The characters were well-developed, and I appreciated the unique perspective of the nonbinary protagonist. However, the romance subplot felt unnecessary and detracted from the overall narrative.

**Writing and Language:**
The writing was engaging, and the author's use of the nonbinary protagonist was commendable. However, the inclusion of a romance subplot felt out of place and diminished the story's impact.

**World-Building and Setting:**
The concept of time manipulation was intriguing, but the execution fell short. The time machine felt underdeveloped, and the scientific explanations lacked depth.

**Themes:**
The story's exploration of time manipulation and its consequences was intriguing, but the execution fell short. The scientific explanations lacked depth, and the time machine felt underdeveloped.

**Emotional Impact:**
The story's emotional impact was diminished by the inclusion of a romance subplot, which felt unnecessary and out of place.

**Enjoyment and Engagement:**
I found the story engaging, but the inconsistent pacing and predictable plot twist left me feeling somewhat disappointed.

**Expectation Fulfillment:**
I was initially excited about the book, but the inconsistent pacing and predictable plot twist left me feeling let down.

### Overall Assessment:
Overall, I found the story engaging, but the inconsistent pacing, predictable plot twist, and unnecessary romance subplot left me feeling somewhat disappointed. While the concept of time manipulation was intriguing, the execution fell short. I would have enjoyed the book more without the romance subplot and with a more developed time machine.

### Score: 3.5

### Review:
**Plot and Structure:**
The story follows Valerie, a park ranger, and her son as they uncover the truth behind a missing hiker and a long-buried secret. Meanwhile, in 1909, Olive and Nessa, two young girls, flee their abusive home and embark on a perilous journey to the Winding Stair Mountains. The dual timelines are expertly woven, creating a seamless narrative that keeps me engaged from start to finish.

**Characters:**
Valerie and Olive are well-developed characters, each navigating their own struggles and triumphs. The supporting cast, including Valerie's son and the girls they meet on their journey, adds depth and complexity to the story.

**Writing and Language:**
The author's writing is both engaging and accessible, making the story easy to follow and enjoy.

**World-Building and Setting:**
The Oklahoma setting is vividly described, immersing readers in the story's world. The contrast between the rugged mountains and the park's beauty adds depth to the narrative.

**Themes:**
The novel explores themes of resilience, courage, and the power of community. It highlights the importance of standing up for oneself and others, even in the face of adversity.

**Emotional Impact:**
The story is emotionally resonant, particularly in its portrayal of Olive and Nessa's journey. Their struggles and triumphs evoke strong feelings of empathy and admiration.

**Enjoyment and Engagement:**
I thoroughly enjoyed this novel and found it hard to put down. The engaging plot and well-developed characters kept me invested throughout.

**Expectation Fulfillment:**
I was drawn to this novel because of its intriguing premise and the author's reputation for crafting compelling stories. The dual timelines and Oklahoma setting further piqued my interest.

### Overall Assessment:
Shelterwood is a captivating novel that combines a gripping plot, well-developed characters, and a richly described setting. The dual timelines and themes of resilience and courage make it a memorable read. I highly recommend this book to anyone looking for a story that is both emotionally resonant and engaging.

### Score: 4.5

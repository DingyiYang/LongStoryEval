### Review:
**Plot and Structure:**
The story's pacing was uneven, with some sections feeling rushed and others dragging on. The mystery was intriguing, but the resolution felt rushed and lacked the satisfying conclusion I had hoped for.

**Characters:**
The characters were well-developed and engaging, but their relationships felt rushed, particularly the romance between Saffron and Alexander. The introduction of Nick Hale added complexity, but his character development was lacking.

**Writing and Language:**
The writing was engaging, and the author's use of botanical metaphors added a unique and enjoyable touch.

**World-Building and Setting:**
The 1920s London setting was vividly portrayed, and the inclusion of real-life historical figures added depth and authenticity.

**Themes:**
The story effectively explores themes of love, friendship, and the challenges of navigating complex relationships.

**Emotional Impact:**
The story elicited strong emotions, particularly in the scenes involving Saffron's family and her relationship with Alexander.

**Enjoyment and Engagement:**
The story was engaging and kept me hooked, but the uneven pacing and rushed resolution left me wanting more.

**Expectation Fulfillment:**
The book's premise was intriguing, and the inclusion of real-life historical figures added an exciting layer of authenticity.

### Overall Assessment:
Overall, I enjoyed the book's premise, characters, and setting, but the uneven pacing and rushed resolution left me wanting more. Despite these issues, the story was engaging, and I'm excited to see where the author takes the series next.

### Score: 3.5

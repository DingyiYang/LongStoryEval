### Review:
**Plot and Structure:**
The story's pacing was uneven, with some sections feeling rushed and others dragging on. The mystery surrounding the murder was intriguing, but the resolution felt rushed and lacked sufficient buildup.

**Characters:**
The characters were well-developed and engaging, but their relationships felt superficial, lacking the depth needed to fully connect with them.

**Writing and Language:**
The writing was engaging, and the author's use of descriptive language effectively brought the setting to life.

**World-Building and Setting:**
The author's vivid descriptions of the setting and the characters' lives created a rich and immersive atmosphere.

**Themes:**
The novel explores themes of friendship, resilience, and the challenges faced by women in the 1950s, which are both relevant and engaging.

**Emotional Impact:**
The story elicited a range of emotions, from joy to sadness, and kept me invested in the characters' lives.

**Enjoyment and Engagement:**
I thoroughly enjoyed the novel and found it to be a delightful read.

**Expectation Fulfillment:**
The novel's premise was intriguing, and I was excited to dive into the story.

### Overall Assessment:
Overall, I enjoyed the novel for its engaging writing, vivid setting, and well-developed characters. However, the uneven pacing and rushed resolution of the mystery left me wanting more. Despite these flaws, the story was a delightful read, and I would recommend it to fans of historical fiction and mystery.

### Score: 3.5

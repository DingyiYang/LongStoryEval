### Review:
**Plot and Structure:**
The story's pacing was uneven, with some sections feeling rushed and others dragging on. The plot twist involving the mother's true intentions was predictable, and the resolution felt rushed and unsatisfying.

**Characters:**
Cordelia's character development was inconsistent, with her growth feeling rushed and underdeveloped. The supporting characters lacked depth, making it difficult to connect with them.

**Writing and Language:**
The writing style was inconsistent, with some sections feeling overly descriptive and others lacking detail. The dialogue was often cringe-worthy and unrealistic.

**World-Building and Setting:**
The world-building was lacking, with the setting feeling underdeveloped and the magic system poorly explained.

**Themes:**
The story's themes of control and manipulation were intriguing, but they were not explored as deeply as they could have been.

**Emotional Impact:**
The story failed to evoke the intended emotional response, leaving me feeling disconnected and uninvested.

**Enjoyment and Engagement:**
I found myself bored and disengaged from the story, struggling to stay interested.

**Expectation Fulfillment:**
The book's premise was intriguing, but it failed to deliver on its potential.

### Overall Assessment:
Overall, I was disappointed by this book. Despite its intriguing premise, the story fell short in terms of pacing, character development, world-building, and emotional impact. The writing style was inconsistent, and the plot twist was predictable. While the themes of control and manipulation were interesting, they were not explored as deeply as they could have been. I would not recommend this book to others.

### Score: 2.5
